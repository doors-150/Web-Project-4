Everything about Naawi-Oodena

This page contains everything about the urban reserve, from the types of housing available to every stage of development. Development started on April 16th, 2024 and ended on October 26th, 2040. All information about the construction will be here.

Low Density Residential

Construction of lower density houses were a part of the first phase of the Naawi-Oodena construction. Most of the low-density areas were developed by the CLC (Canada Lands Company).

These lower-density houses were all made on the left side of the land. With beautiful single-family homes, visually appealing semi-detached homes, duplexes and high-quality townhouses, this area blends in with the rich neighborhood of Tuxedo, which is right beside the urban reserve. The maximum height of any building in this area is 3.5 storeys (12 meters). If you like having a ton of space and nobody to bother you, then this is the perfect place to live.

Medium Density Residential

Construction of the majority of houses here are medium-density. They cover multiple parts of Naawi-Oodena and were developed by both CLC and Treaty 1 Development Corp.

Making up the majority of houses on this land, you can find low-to-mid rise apartments, multi-unit buildings, stacked townhomes, and many live work units. This area provides high-quality, diverse options for housing, for everybody. The maximum height for the houses here is 6 storeys (20 meters), and most buildings contain 30 to 60 units per acre. There are also small convenience stores in these areas. If you like having a little more affordable housing, along with a bit of walkability, then this is a good place to live.

Mixed-use Village

Construction of the Mixed-use Village is a combination of high-density housing with commercial service, retail, and other pedestrian-friendly environments. This area covers a small part of the land, mostly along Grant Ave. Just like medium density, it was equally developed by both companies.

This small area plays a massive part in the urban reserve. It has a mix of uses, like commercial, office and cultural. There are also high-quality multi-unit buildings to live in. This place looks amazing visually, and is very walkable too. Just like the medium-density area, it has diverse options for housing. The maximum height for buildings here is the same as Medium-density (6 storeys, 20 meters), and these have 40-60 units per acre. There are no car sales, services, washes, gas stations, or drive-thrus here, but there is a rapid-transit station. If you love having everything obtainable within a few-minute walk, then this place is perfect.

Commercial Mixed-use

Commercial Mixed-use is where most retail services, offices, and cultural facilities are. It covers most of the area around Route 90 (Kenaston Blvd) and was mostly developed by T1DC (Treaty One Development Corporation).

This takes up the majority of the land, and most of these places allow motorists, pedestrians and cyclists to access them. All buildings look good and are high-quality. The kinds of buildings that are in this area include but are not limited to:

  • Retail Stores
  • Offices
  • Restaurants
  • Personal Services
  • Food Stores
  • Pharmacies
  • Hotels
  • Cultural Facilities
  • Recreational Facilities
  • Entertainment Facilities
  • Gas Stations
  • Charging Stations (for electric cars)
  • And a few residential buildings.
The maximum height of all buildings here is 6 storeys (22.5 meters), and there are almost no houses here.

Community Spaces

The Community Spaces have public parks, open spaces and other outdoor community areas where you can go. This area is in between the lower density, medium density, and mixed-use village neighborhoods so everyone can access these community spaces. It was equally developed by the two companies.

Along with being public places, these community spaces also show some indigenous culture, like their art. These spaces are also be used as gathering places, and have plant species and materials native to Manitoba. The community spaces look beautiful and are sustainable, while also being flexible and being able to be used all-year.

Sports and Recreation

The Sports and Recreation Facility is a space where you can go for recreation and playing sports. It covers a small part of land and is fully developed by Treaty 1 Development Corp.

These recreation facilities are used for both indoor and outdoor use. They are also used for entertainment and cultural uses, along with some retail and commercial use. Just like the commercial mixed-use area, this place is accessible to motorists, cyclists and pedestrians. A nice area to come to if you like sports and exercise.

Cultural Campus and Education

The Cultural Campus and Education Facility is a space for educating people on indigenous history and culture. It covers a small part of the land around Taylor Ave, and is equally developed by both companies.

This is one of the most important places in this urban reserve. There is an administrative centre for the Treaty One company, which is also used as a meeting space. There will also be a campus here for learning and community buildings, along with museums, galleries, spaces for performing, and public libraries, all to showcase indigenous culture and provide an opportunity for reconciliation. Just like Commercial Mixed-Use and the Sports/Recreation areas, you can access this place no matter what form of transportation you choose.
