Naawi-Oodena

And how it changed Winnipeg into a thriving city

About

Naawi-Oodena is a thriving urban reserve that went through years of legal battles and construction to become a reality. This page covers the history of the land that became Naawi-Oodena and the legal battles that helped make this project possible. For information about the types of construction in this urban reserve, go to the about page.

History

Up until 2004, the current place that is Naawi-Oodena used to be known as the Kapyong Barracks, a place where people in the Canadian Army were. Eventually though, the unit moved to a training base called CFB Shilo, abandoning the barracks. Three years later in 2007, a company called the Treasury Board of Canada attempted to sell the entire 64 hectares of land to the Canada Lands Company, however, it was by law that the indigenous actually were entitled to the land and it should've been given to them. The First Nations people noticed this and filed a lawsuit against the government.



This legal battle was supposed to end in 2009 when the court sided with the indigenous, and prevented the land transfer from happening. But the Treasury Board of Canada refused to give up and kept fighting against this, as they could still get help from the federal government. What the company didn't realize though, is that the First Nations people would not give up either.

Victory

In 2015, Stephen Harper was the prime minister at that time. He "met with local first nations leaders" and decided to stop appealing the Treasury Board of Canada. In 2018, the buildings in the Kapyong Barracks started being demolished and on August 30th, 2019, the government signed a deal that would give the land to the indigenous people. After 10 years, they finally won.

Start Of Development

In March 2021, the Treaty 1 Development Corporation released the Kapyong Barracks Master Plan to the public, which is the document that contains information on all the buildings and houses that will be developed on the land. A month later on April 14th, the name "Naawi-Oodena" was given to this urban reserve.



Finally, on July 20th, 2022, the city of Winnipeg agreed to provide services to Naawi-Oodena and help with developing the master plan. Construction three years later on April 16th, 2024 and ended fairly recently on October 26th, 2040. For more information on the construction, visit the about page.
